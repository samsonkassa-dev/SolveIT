Add a Project

Go to the navigation bar and tap on My Projects. If you can't access the side menu, either you're using a smaller screen device or you have minimized it. In that case, click to toggle the side menu.

By tapping on the My Projects menu option, you'll navigate to Project list page.Tap on . You will be shown a dialog with two sections that have to be filled to create your project, which contains two sections :

Project Details Section
This section contains input for all the basic information about the project . The project name, description ...
Project Proposal Section (not required)
In this section you can upload your project proposal. It is very important that it is detailed as possible so that your mentors and trainers can understand your project provide support accordingly.

Judging Criteria

This judging criteria will explain what to do on the contest and clearly describe the criteria of the judging process. So that participants know how their project will be judged. It will also tell the judging body what to look for and how to assign value or rank. The main subject or subject area is clearly presented by thoughtful placement in the frame. The Jury Members may assign a score of 1-5 based on how well the submission meets the criteria listed on the judging criteria document. Participants are encouraged to meet as many of these criteria as possible, to improve their work and chances of winning. However, it is compulsory to meet these criteria.

FAQ

(Q) File Upload Is Not Working

(ANS) What type of file format are you upploading? Make sure that the format is one of the accepted ones. Is the file size more than 50 MB? Make sure that it is not. Then try it again and please notify us if you still have issues.

(Q) What does it take to compete?

(ANS)
  • An innovative idea that could solve your community problems.
  • No other prerequisites!
  • The competition involves the development of Native Apps, SMS Based Service Codes, Web Apps, emerging technologies, hardware and manufacturing tools.

(Q): Can I compete if I am younger than 18 years old?

(ANS): Yes you can. But, you can't compete as an individual and only as a team. In your team, at least two members must be 18 years old and above. Team leader's and team representatives must also be 18 years old and above, and only team members who are 18 and above will travel to Addis Ababa for the boot camp representing your team.

(Q): Can I participate in the competition if I live outside of the listed 15 cities?

(ANS): Yes you can, select the nearest city for you and register under that hub. Unfortunately, Solve IT will not cover your transportation and other related costs when you travel for training and other events.

(Q): is there award money at the end of the competition?

(ANS): No, we do not offer award money. What we offer is seed money to selected top startups at the end of the national competition. To understand more about our seed fund, please download the Seed Fund document attached in the General Terms and Conditions section which addresses the very same topic, and please read it carefully to understand the difference between award money and seed money.

(Q): should I upload my project proposal when I register for Solve IT 2020?

(ANS): No, proposals are not mandatory for registration. However, you need to submit a clear description of your project to complete the registration process. After the orientation training, you must upload your project proposal within one week.

(Q): Can I participate in Solve IT 2020 if I have been enrolled in either of the previous rounds of Solve IT?

(ANS) ፡ As long as you haven't taken any of the training sessions in the previous rounds, you can participate in Solve IT 2020.

(Q): Can I participate in the competition after the registration deadline?

(ANS) We might accept your late application in the highly unlikely scenario where the number of participants in your area doesn't exceed 100 applicants. However, we recommend you to register before the deadline!

(Q): Is it necessary to compete as a team?

(ANS): No, you can compete as an individual. However, we recommend you to compete as a team for the obvious reason. Remember, the number of team members cannot exceed 7.

(Q): Does the competition provide financial and material support?

(ANS) No, Solve IT does not provide any type of financial or material support. However, Solve IT can help you reach and tap the right stakeholders who can do so!

(Q): Can I appeal for a second vote after the judging panel's decision?

(ANS): No. you can't! Both, the regional and national, competitions' results are final.
